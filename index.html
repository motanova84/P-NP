Core Theorem

Lemma 6.24 establishes a structural information-complexity lower bound for SAT instances with bounded treewidth: 

IC(Π, S) ≥ (κ_Π · tw) / ln n

This inequality, combined with the expander-separator construction, shows that no polynomial-time algorithm can decide all instances of 3-SAT — establishing P ≠ NP as a structural consequence of the treewidth hierarchy.
